Technomancy

or the paradox of productivity

August 2025

Any sufficiently advanced technology is indistinguishable from magic (Arthur C. Clarke, 1968); and

Nobody Ever Gets Credit for Fixing Problems that Never Happened (Repenning & Sterman, 2001)

These two concepts altered my brain chemistry. Although Clarke's law is in reference to science fiction, I see it apply everyday when bridging the gap between technology and humanity. As I approach 10 years in the tech industry I still can't help but feel a sense of mystic wonder - one which may never fade.

I've written this piece as a reflection on my career and my 20s; with the lessons I learned along the way. And of course, this wouldn't be one of my blogs without a companion story.

~In a faraway land~
The Emperor lay his head in his hands as he cast his eyes over the war room table.

Invaders to the North, pirates in the West Sea, revolts in the Eastern Provinces, and famine in the South.

For decades had the Empire been in steady decline, losing land, wealth, and influence to the nations at its borders. Long gone were the golden days of plenty the Emperor had enjoyed as a young prince, replaced only by suffering and strife.

To make matters worse the Emperor suspected dissidents within his ranks. The military commanders who had served his father in the wars of conquest, had all succumbed shortly before the Emperor acceded the throne. The constant influx of new blood left him uneasy. Tension bubbled and sparks would flew at every counsel meeting.

You see, in the Empire, magic was restricted to state use only. The sorcerers who drove the revolution that ushered in the golden era were appointed to the ministry - both to serve their country, and to control them. Today, many of the highly commissioned officers were made up of second and third generation magic-users.

However, over time the Empire's enemeies also grew wiser and more resourceful. They learned to live with, counteract, and even benefit from the fearsome sorcery. Their change in tactics had emboldened their transgressions and threatened the Emperor's claim to power.

Desperate times called for desperate measures.

It was customary for the Emperor to not be involved in daily governing affiars, and he preferred it that way. Alas the situation had reached the point of intervention. His presence at the war council was enough to strike fear into even the most senior statesmen. Letting out a sigh of exasperation, he issued a command to the room.

"This council is clearly not working well together. You shall each give me a single plan, and I shall choose. Behave like your life depends on it."

~The Sorcerer's Solution~
A sorcerer is a magic-user whose power is granted innately, usually hereditary. Brilliant sorcerers are exceptionally rare and most require practice to hone their talent.

The Grand Sorcerer propopsed focusing development into a New experimental field of magic, on the promise that it would revolutionise both military and industrial might.

There were very few sorcerers born with the 'gift' of this affinity, most of whom were already sequestered by the state for other duties. The lack of understanding in this field had been the by-product of competing priorities and its inherent complexity. Even the basic comprehension of this kind of magic relies heavily on the mastery of many others, taking years to accomplish.

The Emperor covertly reassigned most state sorcerers to the research project, leaving operations primarily in the hands of the civil administrators. In the public eye the mages simply vanished overnight, fuelling conspiracies and sentiments of abandonment.

The research however, showed remarkable progress. Within a year the sorcerers made several breakthroughs that brought the arcane magic closer to practicality. As development ramped up and morale wore thin, it became harder and harder for the state to hide the program. And so, the Emperor ordered the sorcerers to launch a demonstration of progress to keep the public at bay.

The frontline of the Northern border war had been steadily encroaching. With the faltering support of the country's agriculture and industry, the Northern defenders ceded city after city. But according to the Emperor - this was all going to change.

In the dead of night the sorcerers launched the a devastaing counterattack in the occupied zone.

It went as quickly as it came, and all that lay waste was destruction and death.

The Northern invaders, scattered and shattered by the unprecedented force, fled rapidly. Word spread quickly about the New Magic's power of obliteration throughout all the lands.

Back in the capital, the Emperor prepared for a victory speech - hailing the sorcerers as saviours. Yet when the time came, his words rang out empty over the crowd.

There were no cheers, just stunned silence. A silence that slowly turned to frustration, a frustration that turned to disgust, and a disgust that turned to fury.

The people harboured feelings of disdain towards the Emperor and his neglect of their real problems. Whist the New Magic had both the ability to create AND destroy, that was wholly unclear to the citizens who only saw it as a far fetched distraction from reality. The Emperor's tone deaf action of scorching the earth in their own annexed Northern province was aptly echoed in the court of public opinion.

Despite the unbound theoretical potential of the New Magic, nothing could save the Emperor from his own blindsightedness and mismanagement.

Without trust in the state, the nation plunged into disarray. The provinces rebelled, and either declared independence or seceded to join neighbouring countries. Although no one dared tried to overthrow the Emperor directly from the fear of the unknown consequences of the New Magic.

Soon the Empire became a husk of its former glory. Without the same chokehold grip the state had on the sorcerers, many fled or were captured. Although ironically, the sorcerers themselves had little interest in politics. They happily continued developing the New Magic across the New World.

~The Wizard's Wish~
A wizard is a magic-user whose power is learned through practice and study. They may have some innate capability or be entirely taught, but are limited to the sophistication of the literature.

The Arch Wizard suggested opening the school of magic to the masses. This would allow the Empire to mobilise conscripts and train reservists for self-defence. His calculations also showed the peacetime benefits of magical training would help to improve productivity and loyalty to the crown.

The Emperor consented, and soon enacted two decrees. The former offering basic magical education for all citizens. The latter mandating military service for all able-minded men of age.

Given the previously reserved prestige and status associated with sorcery, many peasants leapt at the opportunity that magic could afford them. On the other hand, the middle-classes were coerced to enlist more due to the relatively preferential treatment that the mage-corps were afforded. Whatever the reason, people signed up in droves for magic classes, eager to get ahead.

This rapid shift in policy quickly strained the existing wizardry acadamies beyond capacity. Mages from across the Empire were diverted to fulfil the Emperor's decrees. The senior sorcerers, wizards, and warlocks were tasked with military training and higher education in the cities, whereas the apprentices were sent to set up new schools in the provinces.

The benefits of the Open Magic Program were felt within the year. Magical literacy skyrocketed tenfold, and the military bolstered its numbers to follow suit. Through sheer numbers and determination the newly empowered populace drove back the Northern invasion and turned their eyes to the Western Seas.

Armed with the ability to defend themselves independently of the navy, the sailors and traders stood a chance against the pirates. The amount of lost cargoes reduced significantly, as the pirates diverted their attention to other, more vulnerable nations.

All the while, the rebellion in the East lost momentum organically. With a sense of hope and promises of prosperity the people of the East no longer felt overlooked by the Empire. The workers of the East began to band together and form guilds of magecraft to further share magic amongst their people.

However, not everything was perfect. The famine in the heartland of the South had been staved off by imports from the reinvigorated other regions. But the poor harvests and increased shortage of peasant labour condemned the farms to a slow and steady decline...

Twenty years after the Emperor's decrees the Arch Wizard declared the Open Magic Program to be a resounding success. Once a rare sight on the streets of the city, magic was now ubiquitous - but most importantly it was accepted.

But perhaps this very acceptence was to become its downfall.

As time went on, magic not only spread, but it became needed. As a common skill the bar was raised for opportunities that would have previously been granted on a silver platter. A citizen mage had to be exceptional to stand out from the crowd, and even then there was no guarantee of prosperity.

Sorcerers were rewarded for training more wizards, which left little time, motivation and resources for deeper research. Eventually the population reached saturation in magical proficiency. There was little incentive to improve magical understanding, as this posed a threat to the system's very existence.

Fundamentally, the Open Magic Program favoured superficial instruction of basic magic with little to no teaching of the foundations. The quality of education also varied wildly from the prestigious school of magic in the capital, in contrast to those run by the guilds in the East, and vocational outposts in the outer provinces; a long hangover from the seminal decision to send journeymen out to enact the Emperor's will.

The Emperor lived long and comfortably enough to abdicate the throne to his son. Framed as an act of grace, the New Emperor took the throne only to find he had inherited a reign marked by shortcuts and complacency. The Empire was now fully 'magified' propped up by citizens who begrudgingly perpetuated archaic magic - a disaster waiting to happen.

~The Warlock's Wargame~
A warlock is a magic-user whose power is granted by a higher-power. This requires some kind of sacrifice or deal, and can be done by anyone - innately magical or not. The Chief Warlock practices the religion of the Old Gods - which has been outlawed for centuries. This kind of magic gains power from the number of people who feed it. He suggests repealing the laws suppressing pracice of the Old Religion and opening patronage to the masses. The Warlock communions with his patron and negotiates the price of patronage to simply be blind accetpance.( of the terms and conditions) The citizens flock to the old religion like moths to a lightbulb, drawn by its promise of easy solutions and seemingly low price. Crop yields improve, the revolts are suppressed - the army and navy find the strength to smike and keep the invaders and pirates away. Eventually the Old Religion spreads far into the world like wildfire, and the capital becomes a holy city. The Emperor is annointed as a saint and give the mandate to rule in the eyes of the god. It starts to encounter other, conflicing religions that challenge its existence. The nation gets pulled into holy wars far from its own borders born to protect its interests in its Religion. The population is one that does not understand the magic it relies on to live everyday, but accepts it willingly. Life becomes unimaginable without the gods. But god giveth and god taketh away. Without free will the population is at the mercy of the religion, who also have a chokehold on the Throne. The Warlocks gradually become the clandestine ruling class with the Emperor just a puppet. the emperor lays his head in his hands and weeps, as he understands why the old religion was outlawed so long ago. e.g. AI, big Tech, social media, religion

If left unchecked each faction will spell the doom of the empire, its citizens, itself or all of the above. The solution lies in keeping all three forever in a tug-of-war with each other. Each faction complements and counterbalances the others + Sorcerers innovate (counters stagnation and dominance) + Wizards evangalise (counters scepticism and deception) + Warlocks capitalise (counters slowness and complacency) Sorcery: Easier to build greenfields instead of fixing the problem at the source.
